Labrador

The Labrador Retriever was bred to be both a friendly companion and a useful working dog breed.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus familiaris
  • Average Length: 56 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 10 – 14 years
  • Habitat: prairies, deserts, grasslands, forests, rain forests, coastal regions and arctic zones

Historically, he earned his keep as a fisherman’s helper: hauling nets, fetching ropes, and retrieving fish from the chilly North Atlantic. Today’s Labrador Retriever is as good-natured and hard working as his ancestors, and he’s America’s most popular breed. These days the Lab works as a retriever for hunters, assistance dog to the handicapped, show competitor, and search and rescue dog, among other canine jobs..
